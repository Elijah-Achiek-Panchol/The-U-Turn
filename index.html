Why We Built The U-Turn

The U-Turn was born out of lived experience, deep frustration and the determination to create change. Our founding team is a diverse collective of women with rich personal stories, professional expertise, and a shared passion for helping others navigate life’s turning points.

Our Story

The U-Turn supports women and youth facing career and life transitions by offering mentorship, practical training, and empowering guidance.

Rooted in the African philosophy of Ubuntu — “I am because we are” — we believe that collective strength, shared growth, and community support are the keys to lasting success.

Our Founder: Rhoda Akuol Philip

Rhoda is a proud South Sudanese-Canadian with a BCom in Technology Management from the University of British Columbia, a Master’s in Public Policy from the University of Toronto, and is currently pursuing her PhD. She has worked across sectors — from startups and nonprofits to academic institutions — and brings a unique lens to career development, systems change, and mentorship. Her commitment to community advocacy is reflected in her service on various advisory boards, including the Pan-Canadian Voice for Women’s Housing (PCVWH).

Each of us has walked the path of immigration and career reinvention. Our education and opportunities in Canada helped us overcome these barriers — now, we're committed to helping others do the same.
